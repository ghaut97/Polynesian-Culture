Family Cultures

Tongan and Samoan families are huge. We each call ourselves sister or brother. We have a lot of family in the islands. My parents family is huge. My grandparents have a big family. My grandmother is a sibling of 15. My grandfather is also a sibling of 15. That’s why we have a lot of family.

Foods

The food is different than American food. Tongan people eat dogs,pigs,squid,octopus,and crab. Samoan food are just the same as Tongan food. We eat a lot of things American people never eat.

Music

The music is the best polynesian culture because it brings everyone together and sing with each other. I will know because i play the ukulele. The music lyrics can be a fast music or a slow music like church music and the fast music can be dancing music. My grandfather and I play together and he was the one who like taught me how to play the ukulele and he influenced me to play and learn.

The clothing is super different. When we dance for someone’s birthday, we make the clothes with mats and we can use it as a mat on the floor. We use the markers or paint. The design’s on the mats means something like of the future.

Culture

Culture is complicated. We do opposite things. When someone dies, we go to the funeral and we talk about the memories but we bring blankets and mats and money. Another one is, when it’s someone’s birthday, we can always dance for the birthday girl/boy and we also give them money. When we have cousins, we don’t call them cousin, we call them “sisters” and “brothers.”
